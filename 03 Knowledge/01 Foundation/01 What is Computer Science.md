# What is Computer Science

## Overview

Computer Science is the study of computation — how problems can be solved using algorithms and data, and how those solutions can be implemented efficiently on machines.

It is not primarily about computers themselves. Computers are just the tool. The real subject is problem-solving, logic, and the structure of information.

## Key Points

- CS divides into theoretical areas (algorithms, complexity, logic) and applied areas (operating systems, networking, databases).
- Everything a computer does reduces to manipulating binary data according to rules.
- The core skill in CS is breaking a large problem into smaller, solvable pieces.

## Example

When you search for something online, CS is involved at every step: the algorithm that finds relevant results, the data structures that store web pages, the network protocols that send data to your screen, and the operating system that runs it all.
